Most students either start too late or try to do everything at once. Both fail. Placements are less about raw effort and more about sequencing the right work in the right weeks. Here is the plan I give every mentee in August of their final year.

## Month 1 — Foundations only

Do not touch competitive programming. Do not start Leetcode Hard. Spend four weeks on:

- Arrays, strings, hash maps (about 30 easy + 30 medium problems)
- One solid side project that actually runs
- Resume version 1 — one page, honest, with numbers

If your fundamentals are shaky here, nothing downstream will hold.

## Month 2 — Patterns, not problems

Switch from "solve this problem" to "recognise this pattern". The highest-leverage patterns:

1. Two pointers and sliding window
2. Monotonic stack
3. BFS / DFS on graphs
4. Dynamic programming on sequences
5. Heaps for top-K and scheduling

Rule of thumb: if you can't name the pattern before writing code, you don't know the problem yet.

## Month 3 — Applied systems + behavioural

This is where most candidates plateau. Add:

- 2 mock system design sessions per week (start small — URL shortener, pastebin, rate limiter)
- 1 behavioural mock per week (STAR format, your own stories)
- Revise CN/OS/DBMS fundamentals, 40 minutes a day

## Month 4 — Mocks every week

By this point, interviews will start. Treat every real interview as a mock too. After each:

- Write down what you fumbled
- Fix one thing in the next 24 hours
- Do not study new topics — only tighten weak ones

## What to skip

- Spending 100+ hours on a 3rd personal project. Polish the one you have.
- Reading competitive programming blogs at 2 AM.
- Optimising solutions *after* solving them, instead of re-solving the next day from scratch.

Placements reward compounding, not cramming. Show up every day, let the plan do the compounding for you.
